Every Student Has a Story Worth Telling

Every young person has a story worth telling. Sometimes they just need help finding it.

The college essay isn't about sounding impressive or getting every word perfect. It's about sounding like yourself and telling your story with confidence, clarity, and heart.

At Essay It Out Loud, I help high school students uncover what makes them unique and craft compelling college essays that stand out for all the right reasons.

The strongest college essays don't sound like everyone else. They sound like the student who wrote them.

My Approach

My mission is simple: to help students discover their voice, tell their story, and submit an essay they can be proud of.

Not an essay written for them. An essay written by them — with expert guidance every step of the way.

Why Me?

I'm an advertising copywriter with nearly 30 years of experience at some of the industry's leading agencies, including Ogilvy and Young & Rubicam.

From television and radio campaigns to digital content and brand storytelling, I've built my career on helping national and global brands discover what makes them different and communicate it clearly.

The same skills that help companies tell their stories help students tell theirs.

I also understand firsthand the pressure students and families can feel. I've guided my own children through the essay-writing process, helping them navigate one of the most personal — and often stressful — parts of the journey.

My role is part sounding board and part editor. Together, we'll uncover the experiences and insights that make your student who they are and shape them into an essay they're proud to submit.

Why “Essay It Out Loud”?

The name Essay It Out Loud comes from one of the simplest and most effective writing techniques I know: read your words out loud.

When you hear your words, you hear the rhythm. You hear where your writing flows and where it doesn't. You hear when you're being yourself and when you're trying too hard.

That's what this process is all about. Finding your voice.

Why Work with Essay It Out Loud?

Every college applicant is competing with strong grades, impressive activities, and long lists of accomplishments.

What admissions officers remember are stories. The best college essays aren't simply about what happened, but what the student learned, noticed, questioned, or came to understand.

The challenge is that many students don't realize they already have one. That's where I come in.

Together, we'll identify the experiences and moments that reveal something meaningful about who they are and shape those ideas into an essay that feels natural, engaging, and distinctly theirs.

The result is an essay that's unmistakably theirs.
